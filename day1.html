Introduction Exercises

Drawing / 2D

Warm up your creative brain with quick sketching games and get to know your fellow campers. We'll move fast, draw loose, and shake off any first-day nerves. No experience needed — this is about loosening up and having fun.

Gartic Phone ↗ garticphone.com The online drawing game we'll use for the intro exercise. No account needed — your instructor will share a room link to join.

How to Think in 3D

Modeling / 3D

Before touching Blender, we train our eyes. Learn to see the world as a collection of shapes, volumes, and interconnected forms. Every chair, face, and building is hiding a box, sphere, or cylinder underneath — this session teaches you to find them.

MAD Arts Tour

General

A guided walkthrough of the MAD Arts space. See past camper work, meet the instructors, learn what's possible, and get oriented for the week ahead.

Becoming the Cube: Intro to Blender

Modeling / 3D

Open Blender for the first time. We'll navigate the 3D viewport, select and move objects, understand the interface layout, and make friends with the default cube. Ian Hubert's rule: everything is just a box — this is where it starts.

Download Blender ↗ blender.org Go to blender.org and click Download Blender — grab the latest stable release for your system (Windows, Mac, or Linux). Run the installer, open it, and you're in. No account, no license, no cost. Blender is free and open source. Photopea ↗ photopea.com A free, browser-based image editor for creating and editing textures. Works like Photoshop — use it to paint, adjust, and export image files you can apply directly to your 3D models in Blender. No install needed.
