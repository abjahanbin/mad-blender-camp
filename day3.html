Motion & Capture

← Back to Home

Drawing Exercises

Drawing / 2D

Daily warm-up sketching. Today's focus: gesture drawing and capturing movement in still images. Draw figures mid-action, objects mid-fall. Learn to freeze energy on the page.

Motion & Emotion: History of Animation

General

From flip books to Pixar — a fast-moving survey of how animation evolved from hand-drawn cells to stop motion to modern CGI. We'll look at the principles that make motion feel alive, and why the best animators are really storytellers in disguise.

Photogrammetry and Alternative Modeling

Modeling / 3D

You don't always have to build a model from scratch. Photogrammetry lets you photograph a real-world object from multiple angles and turn it into a 3D mesh. We'll scan objects from the room around us and pull them into Blender.

Make It Move: Animating in Blender

Animation

Keyframe animation fundamentals in Blender. Set position, rotation, and scale keys. Use the timeline and graph editor to control timing and easing. By the end of this session your cube will be doing something — bouncing, spinning, or telling a tiny story.
