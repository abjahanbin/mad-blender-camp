From flip books to Pixar — a fast-moving survey of how animation evolved from hand-drawn cells to stop motion to modern CGI. We'll look at the principles that make motion feel alive, and why the best animators are really storytellers in disguise.

Animation

Your First Keyframe Animation
01
Select your object
Left-click the object you want to animate. Make sure the timeline at the bottom shows Frame 1.
Left Click
02
Insert your first keyframe
Press I and choose Location, Rotation & Scale. A yellow diamond appears on the timeline — this locks in your first pose.
I → Location, Rotation & Scale
03
Jump forward in time
Click a later frame on the timeline or step forward with the arrow keys. Try Frame 24 — that's one second at 24fps.
→ or click the timeline
04
Move your object to a new pose
Grab, rotate, or scale your object into its second position. This is where Blender will animate toward.
G R S
05
Insert another keyframe and play
Press I again to lock in the new pose. Hit Space to watch Blender animate between them. You just made your first animation.
I then Space
