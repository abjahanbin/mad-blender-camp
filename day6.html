Camera Animation & Compositing

Animation

The camera is a storytelling tool, not just a window. Learn to animate camera movement — dollies, orbits, focus pulls, and handheld shake — to give your scene a point of view. We'll also explore Blender's compositor to layer effects, adjust color grading, add glow and depth of field, and give your renders a final cinematic look.

Camera Path Animation
01
Add a curve path
Add a Path or Bezier curve to the scene. This becomes the rail your camera travels along. Reshape it in Edit Mode to define the camera's journey.
Shift A → Curve → Path
02
Parent the camera to the path
Select the camera first, then Shift+click the curve to add it to the selection. Parent with Follow Path — the camera will lock to the curve.
Ctrl P → Follow Path
03
Fix the camera orientation
On the camera's Follow Path constraint, set Forward to -Z and Up to Y. This ensures the camera points forward along the path rather than sideways or upside down.
Object Properties → Constraints → Follow Path
04
Add a Track To target
Add an Empty at your subject's position. Give the camera a Track To constraint targeting that Empty — now the camera looks at your subject no matter where it is on the path.
Add Constraint → Track To → set To: -Z, Up: Y
05
Animate the offset
Keyframe the Offset value on the Follow Path constraint at frame 1 (value 0) and at your end frame (value 100). The camera smoothly travels the full length of the path.
I over the Offset field to keyframe
Compositor Basics
01
Open the Compositor
Switch to the Compositing workspace at the top of the screen. Check the "Use Nodes" box. You'll see the default setup: Render Layers → Composite. Everything you add goes between these two nodes.
Compositing workspace → Use Nodes
02
Preview as you work
Ctrl+Shift+click any node to route it through the Viewer node and see its output live. Add a Viewer node manually if you want to monitor multiple points in your chain at once.
Ctrl Shift Left Click on any node
03
Color grade with RGB Curves
Add an RGB Curves node between Render Layers and Composite. Lift the shadows, pull the highlights, or push a color tint. This is the fastest way to give a render a distinct look.
Shift A → Color → RGB Curves
04
Add Glare for bloom and streaks
A Glare node adds glow around bright areas — essential for light sources, neon, fire, or anything that should feel like it's actually emitting light. Use the Bloom type for a soft halo, Streaks for a cinematic lens flare.
Shift A → Filter → Glare
05
Less is more
Compositing can quickly become a rabbit hole. Pick one or two effects that serve the story of the scene. A subtle color grade and a hint of glare will do more than stacking fifteen filters.
Keep the node tree readable — label your nodes with Ctrl+F2

Intro to Sound Design with Ableton

Sound Design

Your first session in Ableton Live. Learn the interface, navigate the Session and Arrangement views, and work with clips, samples, and loops. Understand the fundamentals of a DAW — tracks, patterns, MIDI, and the timeline. By the end of the session you'll have a short audio piece playing — something that could soundtrack a scene.
